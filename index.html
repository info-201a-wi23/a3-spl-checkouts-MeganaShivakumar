A3: SPL Library Checkouts

Introduction

I chose to analyze the dataset that contained information about items checked out at least 10 times a month from 2017 - 2023. I thought this would be really interesting to look at to see what books have been popular within the Seattle population for a longer period of time. By understanding this information, I can better illustrate which types of media and of which subject category are most popular within people living in this area. This information can be utilized in the future to better inform what types of products the library invests in and how to improve the products and services based on what people typically enjoy.

Summary Information

Overall, this dataset has 816,354 items checked out including ebooks, books, magazines, movies, and comics, to name a few. The most common method of checking out was by using OverDrive, a library company that digitally distributes ebooks, audiobooks, and online magazines. Over the course of 2017 - 2023, the item that was checked out the most number of times was So You Want to Talk about Race by Ijeoma Oluo. This is a non - fiction book published in 2018 that discusses race in contemporary America. Considering the vast amounts of protests related to police brutality and Black Lives Matter that began in 2020, I believe that this makes sense. However, in contrast to the genre of the most commonly checked out item, the category that had the most number of items checked out within it is comedy, spoofs, and satire. In a way, this also makes sense, as when the Covid - 19 pandemic began, people turned towards comedy and humor in order to cope with quarantine. In addition, the publisher that made the most number of checked out items was Entertainment One Films Canada, which was founded in 2007 and purchased by Hasbro in 2019. Finally, in 2017 the most common type of media checkout was videocasettes and video discs, while the most common type of media checkout in 2023 is ebooks.

The Dataset

This data is collected by the Seattle Public Library, the public library system serving Seattle, Washington that was founded in 1890. This dataset includes information about items checked out at least 10 times per month from 2017 - 2023. The checkout items include items like ebooks, books, magazines, movies, CDs, television dics, and audiobooks, though this is not an exhaustive list. This data was collected through the Open Data program generated by the City of Seattle. This program aims to provide open access to data with the hope of improving the quality of life for residents, promoting economic development and enrichment, and to identify opportunities to better serve the public. When looking at this data, I do not believe that there are any ethical questions that need to be addressed. However, I think it is important to think critically when it comes to implementing the conclusions that are generated using this data. When utilizing this data and the conclusions, it is important to think about how library resources are utilized by different groups of people, and implement policies and products that continue to be equitable to all Seattle residents. Finally, the possible limitations that come with this data include the inability to see at what point in the month people checked out items, whether that be at the beginning, middle, or end of the month.
